Banned Contemporary Adult Fiction

Contemporary novels continue to face censorship challenges, often for addressing current social issues, depicting diverse experiences, or including content deemed sexually explicit or politically controversial.

The Handmaid's Tale
by Margaret Atwood (1985)
Challenged for: Sexual content, anti-Christian themes, profanity

This dystopian novel about a totalitarian theocracy that subjugates women has been challenged for its frank depictions of sexual violence and what some view as anti-religious content, though Atwood has stated the novel is a warning about extremism, not a critique of faith itself.

Beloved
by Toni Morrison (1987)
Challenged for: Violence, sexual content, depiction of bestiality

Morrison's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel about the psychological trauma of slavery has been challenged for its intense depictions of violence and sexual abuse, despite being widely considered one of the most important American novels.

The Kite Runner
by Khaled Hosseini (2003)
Challenged for: Sexual violence, offensive language, religious viewpoint

This novel about friendship and redemption in Afghanistan has been challenged for its depiction of sexual assault, particularly of a child, and for its portrayal of Afghan culture and Islam.

Thirteen Reasons Why
by Jay Asher (2007)
Challenged for: Discussion of suicide, sexual assault, drug use

This novel about a teen's suicide and the events leading to it has been challenged for potentially encouraging suicide, despite many mental health professionals and educators viewing it as an opportunity to discuss teen mental health.

Speak
by Laurie Halse Anderson (1999)
Challenged for: Sexual content, profanity, discussion of rape

This acclaimed novel about a high school student dealing with the aftermath of sexual assault has been repeatedly challenged, despite many survivors crediting the book with helping them process their own experiences and find their voice.
