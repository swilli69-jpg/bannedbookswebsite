Books with LGBTQ+ Themes

Books featuring LGBTQ+ characters or themes face disproportionate censorship. According to PEN America, 41% of books banned in the 2021-2022 school year contained LGBTQ+ characters or themes. These challenges deny LGBTQ+ youth the opportunity to see themselves reflected in literature and prevent all readers from accessing diverse perspectives.

Gender Queer: A Memoir
by Maia Kobabe (2019)
Challenged for: LGBTQ+ content, sexual content, not suited for age group

This graphic memoir about the author's journey with gender identity has been the most challenged book in America for multiple years. It has been targeted in coordinated campaigns despite being placed in high school libraries and addressing common questions about gender.

All Boys Aren't Blue
by George M. Johnson (2020)
Challenged for: LGBTQ+ content, sexual content, profanity

This memoir exploring the author's childhood and adolescence as a Black queer person has been targeted in numerous states. Johnson has spoken out about how banning their book silences Black queer voices and denies LGBTQ+ youth of color the representation they need.

The Perks of Being a Wallflower
by Stephen Chbosky (1999)
Challenged for: Homosexuality, sexual content, drug use

This coming-of-age novel featuring LGBTQ+ characters has been challenged for years, with particular focus on its positive portrayal of a gay character and frank discussions about sexuality and identity.

This Book Is Gay
by Juno Dawson (2014)
Challenged for: LGBTQ+ content, sexual content, political viewpoint

This non-fiction guide for LGBTQ+ teens has been challenged despite providing factual information about sexual orientation and gender identity. The book addresses common questions from teens exploring their identity.

I Am Jazz
by Jessica Herthel and Jazz Jennings (2014)
Challenged for: Transgender character, age-inappropriate sex education

This picture book based on the real life of transgender activist Jazz Jennings has been challenged in elementary schools despite its age-appropriate content simply explaining what it means to be transgender.

George (now published as Melissa)
by Alex Gino (2015)
Challenged for: Transgender character, sexual content (none present)

This middle-grade novel about a transgender girl has been one of the most challenged books despite containing no sexual content. It simply tells the story of a young person understanding and expressing their gender identity.

Fun Home: A Family Tragicomic
by Alison Bechdel (2006)
Challenged for: LGBTQ+ content, sexual content, unsuited for age group

This acclaimed graphic memoir about Bechdel's relationship with her gay father and her own coming out has been challenged in college classrooms, sparking debates about intellectual freedom in higher education.
